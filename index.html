Creating video checklist




Before you Start

These are merely suggestions for your workflow. This list is meant for instructors working on designing a course that incorporates multimedia elements. Checkboxes are persistent (they won’t uncheck on refresh).

Script

Storyboarding is an essential step in the planning process. Storyboarding helps in organizing content and a script helps to produce the best audio by allowing you to avoid mistakes or saying um or uh. Having a scene list is also helpful so that certain segments can be recorded before and after.

Visuals

Combine images, video, and screen recordings together for accompanying the video. Make sure your PowerPoint or Keynote size matches your aspect ratio. For example, on 16:9 widescreen presentation, use a 1280×720 Keynote template. For traditional 4:3 productions, use a 1024×768 template. This ensures there are no letterboxing.

Camera

Do a quick test recording to make sure the settings are set and play back. Be sure to record at an appropriate resolution so as not to fuzzy/pixelated and positioned correctly.

Microphone

Do a quick test recording to check any background noise issue. Make sure not to have any items such as paper near the microphone.

Lights

Have adequate lighting. Particularly, try to have more light in front of you than behind you. Otherwise you may be shadowed badly.

Close captioning

Adding closed captions to videos is a basic requirement to meet accessibility standards. You can easily do so by using YouTube's Automatic captions. Please note that the the quality of the captions varies, so after enabling automatic captions, always edit the captions to improve accuracy and available to viewers.

Amara is a free and open source captioning, subtitling, and translation tool. Amara allows manually type in your captions and sync them with your video.

Be loose

Talking with your hands helps to keep the energy up and feels more natural for the speaker and audience. Sometimes cracking a tiny smile as you're reading lines will help to keep the tone light and conversational. Don't take yourself too seriously! If you mess up, shake it out and try it again as it’s not a big deal. It’s okay to do multiple takes. Trust that what you shoot will get edited. So read the line until it feels right. Have a friend encourage you to be great. Encouragement and constructive criticism is very helpful from behind the camera.
